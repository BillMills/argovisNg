What am I looking at?

You are looking at a map of Argo profile locations around the globe in a three day window that ended yesterday.

Argo profiles are the dots peppering the map. They measure temperature, pressure and salinity only. Clicking on a profile opens a popup with more information, links, and features.

Yellow dots are standard Argo floats. They measure temperature, pressure and salinity only.
Green dots are BGC profiles, they include Biological measurements as well, such as dissolved oxygen, chlorophyll, and more!
Blue dots represent deep profiles, they dive as much as 6000 meters below the surface. Wow!

Can I change the date from yesterday to something else?

You sure can. The sidebar to the left has a date input element, pictured below.

What is that pentagon in the upper left corner?

Oh that? That allows you to draw spherical polygons on the map, which lasso profiles falling within its area. Completing a shape opens another pop-up that gives options to view all available profiles with or without the pressure range applied. The dates these profiles were taken is selected by the date range input pictured below.

The pressure range bar filters out profiles that don't fall within that range.

By the way, try using different projections. Selecting the projection in the drop down menu on the top right corner is how its done.

What do those other icons do?

The upside down triangle opens a bottom sheet window with database information. including: latest profile in the database, number of profiles in database, number of deep profiles, number of BGC profiles, and a list of all the Data Aquisition Centers (DACS) that report Argo profiles

The next icon (tiles with the slash) will clear all profiles and drawn items from the map. Things can get cluttered, This button is used to clear everything.

The reset button clears drawn shapes and returns the page's state back to its default view. Think of it as a 'startover button'
